About the Simulation

This simulation demonstrates the ideal gas law: P × V = n × R × T, where P is pressure, V is volume, T is temperature, n is the number of moles, and R is the gas constant. For simplicity, we keep n and R constant, so our relationship becomes P × V = T.

Boyle's Law

For constant temperature (T), pressure and volume are inversely proportional:

P × V = constant

Charles's Law

For constant pressure (P), volume is directly proportional to temperature:

V / T = constant

Gay-Lussac's Law

For constant volume (V), pressure is directly proportional to temperature:

P / T = constant
