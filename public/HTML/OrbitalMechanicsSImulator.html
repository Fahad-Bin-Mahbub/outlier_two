Understanding Orbital Mechanics

Kepler's Laws

  • 1 Planets move in elliptical orbits with the Sun at one focus.
  • 2 A line joining a planet and the Sun sweeps out equal areas in equal times.
  • 3 The square of the orbital period is proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis.

Orbit Types

Circular (e = 0) Special case of an ellipse where the foci coincide.
Elliptical (0 < e < 1) Closed orbit with varying distance from the central body.
Parabolic (e = 1) Escape velocity with exactly enough energy to escape the gravitational field.
Hyperbolic (e > 1) Open orbit with excess velocity to escape the gravitational field.

Key Factors Affecting Orbits

Mass

Larger central body mass creates stronger gravity, affecting orbital velocity requirements and period.

Velocity

Initial velocity determines if an orbit is circular, elliptical, parabolic, or hyperbolic. Escape velocity is key.

Distance

Initial distance affects required velocity for stable orbit and helps determine orbital energy.

About This Simulator

This orbital mechanics simulator provides an interactive way to explore how different parameters affect the behavior of orbiting bodies. The simulation uses real physics equations to calculate orbital trajectories based on gravitational forces.

While simplified for educational purposes, this simulator demonstrates the fundamental principles of orbital mechanics that govern the motion of planets, moons, and satellites in our solar system and beyond.

Simulation Notes:

  • Gravitational calculations use Newton's law of universal gravitation
  • The simulation uses a 2D representation of orbital paths
  • Central body is fixed in place and rotation effects are not simulated
  • Relativistic effects are not included in this basic model
